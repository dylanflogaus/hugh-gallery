The Artist

Painter of light
and colour

Hugh Phibbs is a contemporary watercolour and abstract artist based in the Pacific Northwest. His work explores the relationship between colour, light, and the natural world — finding beauty in the quiet, the fleeting, and the overlooked.

Born and raised in a family of painters, Hugh grew up watching his mother work at her studio easel. He studied fine art at the Pacific Northwest College of Art before spending several years in Europe absorbing the light of Provence and the rain-soaked gardens of the English countryside.

"I want people to feel like they've stepped into a garden after rain — that particular sense of stillness and heightened colour."

— Hugh Phibbs

The Practice

How the work comes to be

Hugh's process is slow and considered — he often spends weeks on a single piece, layering washes until the canvas feels alive. He works from observation as much as from intuition.

His studio is a converted boathouse overlooking Puget Sound, surrounded by wild roses, foxglove, and ancient rhododendrons. The garden feeds directly into the work.

01

Observing

Mornings in the garden or on long walks — sketching, taking notes on light and colour with a small watercolour kit.

02

First Wash

Laying broad, wet-on-wet washes to establish the mood and major colour relationships. This stage must be spontaneous.

03

Building Layers

Glazing transparent layers over weeks, deepening shadow and lifting light. Patience is the main tool.

04

Knowing When to Stop

The hardest part. Overworked watercolour loses its luminosity. Hugh stops before the painting feels 'finished.'

Materials & Methods

Cotton Rag Paper

Sable Brushes

Pastel & Mixed

Unison soft pastels and inks introduced in later layers for texture and luminous accents.
