The Artist

Hugh C. Phibbs

Hugh Phibbs is an accomplished artist based in Swarthmore, Pennsylvania, with over forty years of experience creating and preserving art. He holds a Master of Fine Arts from the University of Michigan and has taught studio classes there and through the Smithsonian Resident Associates' Program in Washington, DC. He spent more than thirty-five years specializing in paper preservation as a member of the Conservation Division at the National Gallery of Art, and has lectured regularly on paper preservation at Winterthur, Buffalo State University, and the Institut National du Patrimoine in Paris. His work has been exhibited at the Protetch/Macintosh Gallery in Washington, the Ellipse Gallery in Arlington, VA, and during Wilmington, DE's Art Loop. In 2017, he was named the established visual art fellow.

"My oil and watercolour works feature figures, flowers, and landscapes — connecting these subjects with contemporary perspectives through the interplay of background, gesture, and colour."

— Inspired by Cézanne, Marin & Johns

Hugh divides his time between Swarthmore, Pennsylvania — where he lives with his wife Joan, a dancer and choreographer, next to his daughter Emma (owner of Move Well PT) and her family — and Boothbay Harbor, Maine, where the coastal environment serves as a source of artistic inspiration through the summer months. His son Liam, an attorney at Hogan Lovells in Washington, D.C., lives with his family near Annapolis, Maryland.

The Practice

Hugh's process with watercolour

Most of these images are painted on Arches cold-press paper with Golden QoR watercolour — a synthetic medium made of Aquazol and pigment. They reflect a belief that water is an incredibly powerful molecule, capable of far more than I ever could.

My goal is to let the water spread colour naturally, guiding it only enough to suggest form — so I can learn to observe and create without letting ego interfere.

A handful of works are painted on handmade paper from Two Rivers Paper in Somerset, England. They've temporarily paused shipping to the US, so pieces on their paper are limited.

01

Arches Cold-Press

A sturdy, absorbent surface that lets the washes pool, bloom, and settle into soft, living edges.

02

Golden QoR

An Aquazol-based watercolour — pigment in a modern synthetic binder that stays luminous and re-workable.

03

Letting Water Lead

Flooding the page and letting the pigment find its own edges. The water does most of the work.

04

A Lighter Hand

Stepping in only enough to suggest form — observing and creating without letting ego interfere.
